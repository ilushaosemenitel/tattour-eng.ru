Kazan

— Kazan is the capital of the Republic of Tatarstan, a major port on the left bank of the Volga River, and one of the largest religious, economic, political, scientific, educational, cultural, and sports centers in Russia. The city has a registered brand called "the Third Capital of Russia."

It was founded in the early 11th century,
and its millennium was celebrated in 2005.

Day 7: Nizhnekamsk — Kazan

Return trip with final walks in Kazan.

How to get there: Car / Taxi: - The distance is approximately 200 km, and the travel time is about 2-2.5 hours. - You can rent a car or take a taxi.



Public transportation: - There are regular buses or trains, but it is better to use a car or taxi for flexibility.

Transport summary

- It is recommended to rent a car or use a taxi for the entire trip — this is the most convenient and flexible way.
- In Kazan, walking and public transport are convenient inside the city.
- Long-distance travel — preferably by private vehicle or taxi: it's faster and more comfortable this way.

- Taxi: Use the Yandex. Taxi or Gett apps, which are widely available.
- Car: Consider local or international rental companies (Avis, Europcar) for car rentals.
- Plan ahead: Especially for intercity travel.
